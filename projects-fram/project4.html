Project 4

The Assignment

This project was all about CNC routers. We had to design an object, model it in 3D, put through Vcarve and route it out of plywood. I was in charge of design and modeling, Sigurður of setting up Vcarve and Baldur took care of the physical routing. Their pages can be found here Sigurður and Baldur, were they go deeper into the individual processes. As for the design and modeling, that process can be read about here below.

Design and modeling

We were quick to decide that a flatpack would be easiest and the most time conserving. That is no difference in the cut depth. After having talked it over a bit we realised that we all could some use out of a good chair. Something low and relaxed. I have lived in Denmark for a long time so some sort of scandinavian influenced design was the only option. Luckily it was my job to design it.

After having looked at some classic chairs and a few sketches later I came up with an initial design (left). For the geometry I found this image online (right) which is, I think, from the very reliable book Humanscale 1/2/3 by Niels Diffrient.

Next came the hard part, making somthing that looked good in 3D. For that I used, like always, Solidworks. It took some time, adjusting lines and curves but I'm pretty happy with the outcome.

The last thing on my list was to create a single DXF file with all the cut surfaces. That was done in the same way as with project 2, which you can read about here. I created three different files, one for each thickness of plywood. We had access to 12, 18 and 21mm.

Now that's all for my part of the assignment. So head over to Sigurðurs page to find out about the next part. I do think though that he changed the arragement of the parts to fit two chairs on our 1.5mx1.5m of plywood... ;-)

Like alway the project files can be found in the random files folder in my repository
